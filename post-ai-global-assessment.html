UN Releases First Global Independent Scientific Assessment on Artificial Intelligence

July 2, 2026

UN Expert Panel

This week, an independent expert panel convened by the United Nations officially released the first global scientific assessment report on the opportunities, risks, and societal impacts of artificial intelligence. This marks the international community's first attempt to utilize a unified scientific framework to analyze the profound changes brought by this technology over the past few years.

A striking detail highlighted in the report is the scale of usage: currently, over one billion people worldwide use conversational AI tools every week. From writing and programming to daily consultations, it has permeated the details of ordinary people's lives. Meanwhile, the expert panel issued a warning—the development of AI capabilities has significantly outpaced the scientific community's understanding and governments' capacity to adapt. In other words, technology is running faster than comprehension and regulations.

The report specifically mentions a group that is easily overlooked: children. An increasing number of minors are using AI to complete homework or even seek life advice, prompting educators and parents to rethink how to enjoy the convenience of these tools while preventing children from becoming overly dependent on technology.

Following the report's release, the UN Secretary-General stated that the scientific evidence supporting decision-making is now on the table. The next test is whether governments can swiftly follow up with the formulation of governance rules, rather than waiting for risks to become facts before reacting. Analysts believe this report acts more like an "urgency notice": tech companies, regulatory bodies, and educational systems all need to recalibrate their pace after its publication.
